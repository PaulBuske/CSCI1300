  I experienced a collage of cultures just from walking around the city. All the different customs contrast against the overall lifestyle of what it is to be a "New Yorker". All five senses were uniquely bombarded as we transitioned between boroughs. I found it fascinating.

  It was a humbling and somber experience to visit ground zero six months after the events of 9/11. We were there to visit my sister who was late to work at the trade towers that day. There were signs of the missing, U.S. flags signed by supportive citizens from all over the United States, and recover crews going to and from their shifts. I spoke with a junior captain from one of the fire crews that were lost in the towers. He said that he took shifts helping with clean up on his days off from the fire department. His demonstrated love for his fellow man and city were above reproach. I remember returning to her apartment in New Jersey and watching the spotlights light up for the first time to represent where the towers used to be. The whole the experience influenced my decision to join the Army after high school.
